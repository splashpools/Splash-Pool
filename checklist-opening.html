Pool Opening Checklist

Now, as winter comes to an end, please let us give you a good piece of advice. Take this from the experts!

Spring Preparation

check_circle

If You Own a Mesh Cover

Early March: You must unplug and remove your automatic pump, so your pool can fill back up with rain water.

After this date: Shock your pool once a week with liquid chlorine (especially on colored plaster).

check_circle

If You Own a Solid Cover

It is time now for you to start filling your pool back up.

  • Pump off whatever water excess might be on the cover
  • Inspect the quality of the water in the pool
  • If water is clear, simply proceed to fill pool back up with your garden hose a little bit at a time (especially if you are on a water well)

Pre-Season Services Available

Pre-season services are also available to help your pool minimize the effects of the winter. As well as weekly maintenance services and weekly water test and balance.

Every action you take in the spring will reflect good or bad results upon the pool opening. Let us know how we may assist you.
warning

Important Reminders

  • Water Level: The water level must be at operational level (mid skimmer / ceramic tile) to avoid trip back in case your pool cannot be properly started.
  • Cover Debris: A comprehensive amount of leaves and/or water is removed from covers included with your service. If this amount is excessive, additional fees apply.
  • Hardware Access: Pool side hardware needs to be available or easily accessible for us to be able to install at time of opening.
